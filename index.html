Tie and Dye by Piyush Jain

Natural Colour Effects Using Eco-Friendly Dyes

About Materials Process Result

About the Project

This project demonstrates the traditional tie and dye technique using natural colours obtained from natural sources.

The aim of this project is to create attractive fabric patterns while promoting sustainable and eco-friendly practices.

Materials Used

  • White cotton cloth or T-shirt
  • Rubber bands or thread
  • Bowls and spoons
  • Water
  • Gloves
  • Turmeric for yellow dye
  • Beetroot for pink dye
  • Tea or coffee for brown dye
  • Spinach leaves for green dye
  • Alum or salt as a fixing agent

Procedure

Step 1: Wash the Fabric

Wash the cotton fabric thoroughly and let it dry completely.

Step 2: Tie the Fabric

Fold and tie different sections of the fabric using rubber bands to create patterns.

Step 3: Prepare Natural Dyes

Boil turmeric, beetroot, spinach leaves, tea, or coffee separately in water. Strain the liquid and allow it to cool.

Step 4: Dye the Fabric

Dip different sections of the tied fabric into the natural dyes and leave it for 30 to 60 minutes.

Step 5: Dry and Untie

Allow the fabric to dry completely. Remove the rubber bands and observe the final design.

Final Result

The final fabric displayed vibrant patterns created using natural colours. This project demonstrates that sustainable methods can create beautiful textile designs.
